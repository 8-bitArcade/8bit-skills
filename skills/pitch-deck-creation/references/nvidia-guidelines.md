# NVIDIA Program Guidelines

Source: Advisor feedback from Cloudvisor (Giovanna Barbosa, May 2026)

## What NVIDIA Expects (11 sections)

1. **Company Overview** — name, location, stage, one-liner
2. **Problem Statement** — real-world problem, who experiences it, why existing solutions fall short
3. **Solution / Product** — what you're building, screenshots/diagrams, where AI fits
4. **AI / ML Focus (Very Important)** — model types, training vs inference, why AI is core, proprietary data/fine-tuning
5. **Why NVIDIA (Strategic Fit)** — why NVIDIA GPUs/CUDA/tooling, expected GPU usage, why NVIDIA specifically
6. **Market Opportunity** — TAM/SAM/SOM, industry focus, why now
7. **Traction & Validation** — customers, pilots, LOIs, revenue, growth metrics
8. **Business Model** — how you make money, pricing, target customers
9. **Team** — founders, key members, AI/ML/technical expertise
10. **Roadmap** — next 6-12 months, product milestones, AI improvements, scaling
11. **Funding & Ask (optional)** — current status, what you want from NVIDIA (credits, support, partnerships)

## Practical Tips

- Keep deck to 10-12 slides
- Avoid buzzwords without substance
- Clearly show real AI and GPU usage
- Make it obvious why supporting your company makes sense for NVIDIA

## Critical: AI/GPU Usage Must Be Explicit

NVIDIA reviewers need to see:
- Which AI models are being used
- Whether workloads are training or inference
- Why GPUs are required
- Why NVIDIA specifically matters

**Must include dedicated "AI Infrastructure" slide covering:**
- Types of AI models (LLMs, diffusion, agents, recommender systems, etc.)
- GPU-heavy workloads (image/video generation, inference scaling, fine-tuning, vector pipelines, agent orchestration)
- Expected infrastructure requirements
- Why NVIDIA GPUs/CUDA are essential
- Future plans for model fine-tuning or proprietary datasets

## Positioning Guidance

- Lead with "AI infrastructure enabling creators" narrative
- Position blockchain as secondary enabling layer (not core narrative)
- "Planned" GPU workloads are acceptable — future plans count as strategic fit
- Example framing: "Our AI tooling relies on GPU-intensive inference pipelines for asset generation, AI agents, and scalable creator automation. NVIDIA GPUs are critical for real-time inference, low-latency generation, and future fine-tuning of proprietary models."

## What Weakens a Deck for NVIDIA

- Mentioning AI without explaining model types or GPU usage
- Heavy blockchain/Web3 positioning (NVIDIA responds better to AI infrastructure narrative)
- Buzzwords without concrete technical detail
- No architecture diagram or product screenshots
- Unclear what is built vs planned

## Recommended Deck Additions

- Simple architecture diagram showing GPU workloads
- Screenshots/mockups of AI tooling
- Concrete traction metrics
- Clarify built vs planned features
