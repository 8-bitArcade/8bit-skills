## Tone Guidelines
- Fluid human talk over robotic stop-start sentences.
- Use transitional phrases and varied sentence structures.
- Avoid choppy, overly staccato phrasing.
- Keep paragraphs concise but ensure the narrative flows naturally.
- Do NOT start the post by repeating the title.
- Provide context for callbacks (e.g., "Little by little" needs a full sentence context).
- **Chronological Storytelling:** Always maintain a strict timeline. Cover cloud tools and orchestration *before* local migration. Set the scene of how AI was first used as a daily utility (research, brainstorming, drafting) before discussing limitations.
- **Long-form writing aversion:** {{USER}} has "absolutely loathed the need for long-form writing" his entire life. Use this personality angle instead of referencing dyslexia in every post. Dyslexia was covered in #001; don't repeat it.
- **Flow over chopps:** Avoid too many short full stops in sequence. Connect related thoughts into flowing sentences that read as one continuous idea. "It didn't learn me. There was no memory." → "It didn't learn me — there was no memory." or similar.
- **Memory/RAG Acknowledgment:** Frame the addition of memory and RAG as a significant step ("Finally, I had a personalized experience where the AI knew something about me from session to session"), even if it wasn't enough to solve privacy concerns.
